Overview

International transfer applicants follow the same application as domestic transfers, with additional steps for language proficiency and visa documentation when applicable. Transfer credit is evaluated course-by-course using official transcripts and, when needed, detailed syllabi.

Step-by-step checklist

1) Prepare documentation

  • Official transcripts from all institutions attended. If not in English, include certified translations.
  • Keep syllabi for courses you want evaluated for transfer credit.
  • English proficiency testing (TOEFL, IELTS, or Duolingo) if applicable based on your language background and schooling.

2) Apply

  • Submit the Common App for Transfer by W&M deadlines.
  • Use an email address that reliably receives international mail from U.S. universities.

3) After admission (visa & enrollment)

  • Coordinate financial certification and immigration documents with the appropriate W&M office (e.g., Reves Center) to request your I-20/DS-2019, if needed.
  • Attend orientation and complete any placement testing (e.g., language).

4) Degree planning at W&M

  • Complete at least 60 credits in residence at W&M to graduate.
  • Finish any remaining COLL requirements in residence (e.g., COLL 150, part of COLL 200, COLL 400; plus language/arts proficiencies if not met before matriculation).
  • Work with your advisor to map prerequisites and upper-division major requirements.
