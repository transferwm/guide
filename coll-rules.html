COLL Rules & Requirements for Incoming Transfer Students

The following summarizes the COLL Curriculum Rules and Requirements for transfer students entering William & Mary’s College of Arts & Sciences. These policies apply to all transfer admits, including those from the Virginia Community College System (VCCS), Richard Bland College (RBC), and other accredited institutions. The information below consolidates university policy and EPC-approved amendments effective through Spring 2022 [oai_citation:2‡COLL Curriculum Rules and Requirements for Incoming Transfer Students _ Undergraduate Program _ College of Arts & Sciences _ William & Mary.pdf](sediment://file_00000000138c61f683bedbcfa1217c34) [oai_citation:3‡curriculum-rules-and-requirements-for-transfers.pdf](sediment://file_0000000044b061f6b263c40ca149c3c5).

General Rules for All Transfer Students

  1. COLL 100: Waived for students entering with at least two full-time college semesters post-high school (sophomore/junior standing).
  2. COLL 150: Must be completed at W&M (no transfer credit accepted).
  3. COLL 200: Three courses required in residence—one in each domain (ALV, CSI, NQR); transfer credit not accepted.
  4. COLL 300: May be petitioned for prior study-abroad/study-away experience earning ≥3 transferable credits with grade ≥C.
  5. COLL 350 & 400: Must be fulfilled in residence after matriculation.
  6. Transfer Credit: May apply to proficiency (Math, FLP, Arts) and domain-credit requirements, but not to COLL 150, 200, 350, or 400.
  7. VCCS/RBC AA, AS, or AA&S graduates: Guaranteed junior standing (≥54 credits) per Virginia State Policy on Transfer.
  8. Upper-Division COLL (300+): Not subject to State Policy on Transfer; must be taken at W&M.
