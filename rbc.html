Overview

Students completing a transfer-oriented associate degree at Richard Bland College may transfer to William & Mary under the current RBC–W&M agreement if all stated conditions are met. Admission to specific programs (e.g., Business/Education) may require separate applications or prerequisites.

Step-by-step checklist

1) Confirm your degree plan

  • Pursue an RBC AA or AS that is designated as university-parallel/transfer-oriented.
  • Plan early for your intended W&M major (math, lab science, and sequence prerequisites).
  • Use the COLL lists to select RBC/VCCS courses that satisfy W&M general education areas.

2) Letter of Intent (LOI)

  • Submit the W&M Letter of Intent by the posted deadlines (typically Feb 1 for fall, Sept 1 for spring).
  • Make sure you meet the GPA/credit and course requirements in the current RBC–W&M agreement.

3) Maintain eligibility while finishing the degree

  • Keep your overall GPA at or above the threshold in the agreement through degree conferral.
  • Remain continuously enrolled as specified (summer typically excluded).
  • Earn required in-residence credits at RBC that count toward the degree.
  • Complete any required composition, math, and COLL-area courses with the minimum grades specified by the agreement.

4) Apply to W&M

  • Submit the Common App for Transfer by W&M deadlines.
  • Send official transcripts from all colleges.
  • Provide syllabi if requested for transfer-credit evaluation.

5) After admission (what remains at W&M)

  • Expect junior standing on entry (subject to caps and degree rules).
  • Complete at least 60 credits in residence at W&M to graduate.
  • Finish remaining COLL requirements in residence (e.g., COLL 150, part of COLL 200, COLL 400; plus language/arts proficiencies if not met before matriculation).
  • Follow any major-specific prerequisites and declaration timelines.

Planning tools & references

  • W&M Letter of Intent (LOI)
  • COLL area lists (what courses satisfy MATH, NQR, CSI, ALV, ARTS)
  • Sample RBC/VCCS → W&M equivalencies

Always consult the current RBC–W&M agreement and W&M catalog for definitive requirements.
