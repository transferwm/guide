Welcome

This site consolidates steps, checklists, and links for the main transfer paths to William & Mary. Use the navigation above to jump straight to your path.

  • Know your path: VCCS (GAA), RBC, Other U.S., or International.

Quick start by pathway

VCCS (Guaranteed Admission Agreement)

Complete a qualifying transfer-oriented associate degree, submit the Letter of Intent on time, meet GPA/credit requirements, and apply via the Common App.

Richard Bland College (RBC)

Follow the RBC–W&M GAA, file the Letter of Intent, and apply via the Common App. Some programs (e.g., Business) have separate admissions.

View step-by-step →

Other U.S. Colleges/Universities

Apply via the Common App, send official transcripts, and be ready with syllabi for course-by-course credit evaluation by W&M.

View step-by-step →

International Transfers

Apply via the Common App; English-proficiency testing is strongly recommended for non-native speakers. Course credit is evaluated from syllabi.

View step-by-step →

What an associate’s degree typically covers

  • Junior standing on entry (credit limits apply; program admissions may be separate).
  • Many lower-division general education areas may be satisfied, but you’ll still complete remaining COLL requirements in residence (e.g., COLL 150, part of COLL 200, COLL 400) and at least 60 W&M credits in residence to graduate.

Details vary by pathway and catalog year — verify on official pages.
