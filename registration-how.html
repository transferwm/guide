How to Register

This page walks you through carts → priorities → time tickets → add/drop/waitlist. Dates/times change each term—always confirm on the Registrar site.

1) Build your cart in PATH

  • Log in to PATH and use Class Search to add planned courses to your **Primary Cart**; save alternates in the **Alternatives Cart**.
  • Open each course’s **Registration Options** to set a priority (First, Second, …) and designate up to **two alternatives**; for multi-section courses you can choose “**any other section**.”
  • Add **all co-requisites** (e.g., lecture + lab + discussion); PATH will attempt the entire group at the **highest priority** among them.
  • Resolve **holds** and **registration errors** in the cart before carts close; unresolved issues block processing.

2) Time tickets & “When to Register”

Your **time ticket** determines when PATH attempts to register your cart (Phase 1) and when real-time registration opens. Term outlines post on the Registrar’s **When to Register** page; times are listed in **Eastern Time**.

  • Typical pattern: carts open in late February (fall) or late September (spring); see “When to Register.”
  • New-student example (Fall 2025): during the July add/drop window, **transfer students** began at 11:00 a.m., followed by first-years at 12:00 p.m. (example only; check the current term).

3) Add/Drop and Waitlist

  • During **Add/Drop**, you register in real time in PATH up to the stated credit limit for your group.
  • After the final Add/Drop deadline, schedule changes require a **withdrawal** (resulting in a “W”) and may need approval depending on load.
  • Use PATH’s **waitlist** when available; monitor status and conflicts.
  • Overrides from instructors still require **you** to add the class in PATH; instructors cannot register you.

Pro tips

  • Put **more than the max hours** in your Primary Cart (PATH evaluates round-by-round until you’re registered up to the cap).
  • Use **Auto** priority as a tie-breaker if you set the same number on multiple courses.
  • Start early so you can clear **holds**, request **permissions**, and fix **pre/co-req** issues before carts close.
