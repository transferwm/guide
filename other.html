Overview

This path is for students transferring from accredited U.S. institutions other than VCCS or RBC. There is no guaranteed-admission agreement; applications are reviewed holistically. Transfer credit is evaluated course-by-course by the Registrar.

Step-by-step checklist

1) Plan your courses with the W&M core/major in mind

  • Review W&M general education areas (COLL) and your intended major’s prerequisites.
  • Save syllabi for all courses you hope to transfer—these help with equivalency decisions.
  • Use the COLL lists as a guide to the types of courses that tend to satisfy W&M areas.

2) Apply

  • Submit the Common App for Transfer by W&M deadlines (typically Mar 1 for fall, Oct 1 for spring).
  • Send official transcripts from every college attended (and high school if W&M requires it based on your completed credits).
  • Provide course syllabi promptly if the Registrar requests them.

3) After admission (what remains at W&M)

  • Complete at least 60 credits in residence at W&M to graduate.
  • Finish any remaining COLL requirements that were not satisfied via transfer credit (e.g., COLL 150, part of COLL 200, COLL 400, and language/arts proficiencies if not previously met).
  • Declare your major on the W&M timeline and follow department prerequisites.
