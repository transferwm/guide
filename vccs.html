Overview

William & Mary offers a Guaranteed Admission Agreement (GAA) for students completing an approved, transfer-oriented associate degree at a VCCS college and meeting all requirements. Admission to certain programs (e.g., Business/Education) is separate.

Quick links: Letter of Intent • COLL area lists • Equivalency lookup • Sample equivalencies

Step-by-step checklist

1) Confirm your degree path

  • Choose a transfer-oriented associate degree (AA/AS/AA&S). Only SCHEV-recognized A.S. in General Studies qualify as “transfer” degrees.
  • Plan early for your intended W&M major (math/lab/sequence prerequisites).
  • Use COLL lists to pick VCCS courses that satisfy W&M general education areas (MATH, NQR, CSI, ALV, ARTS).

2) File the Letter of Intent (LOI)

  • Eligible after you complete ≥ 15 post-HS VCCS credits with GPA ≥ 3.4.
  • Deadlines: Feb 1 (for fall entry) • Sept 1 (for spring entry).
  • Submit the LOI: W&M LOI form.

3) Stay GAA-eligible while you finish

  • Maintain GPA ≥ 3.4; remain continuously enrolled (summers excluded).
  • Earn at least 45 post-HS VCCS credits applied to the degree.
  • Grades:
    • B or better in a transferable 200-level ENG.
    • B or better in VCCS courses you’re using for W&M COLL areas (MATH, ALV, CSI, NQR, ARTS).
    • Only courses with C or better transfer for credit.

4) Apply to W&M

  • Submit the Common App for Transfer by Mar 1 (fall) or Oct 1 (spring).
  • Send official transcripts from all colleges; provide syllabi if requested.

5) After you’re admitted

  • Enter with junior standing (credit/degree rules apply).
  • Complete at least 60 W&M in-residence credits to graduate.
  • Finish remaining COLL items in residence (e.g., COLL 150, part of COLL 200, COLL 400; language/arts proficiencies if not met pre-matriculation).
