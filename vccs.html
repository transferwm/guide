Overview

William & Mary offers a Guaranteed Admission Agreement (GAA) for students completing an approved, transfer-oriented associate degree at a Virginia Community College System (VCCS) institution and meeting all GAA requirements. Admission to specific programs (e.g., Business/Education) may require a separate process.

Key tools: Letter of Intent • COLL area course lists • Sample equivalencies • Transfer Virginia program guides

Step-by-step checklist

1) Confirm your degree path

  • Your degree should be an approved, university-parallel AA / AS / AA&S. Only SCHEV-recognized A.S. in General Studies qualify as “transfer” degrees.
  • Plan early for your intended W&M major (math/lab/sequence prerequisites).
  • Use COLL lists to pick VCCS courses that satisfy W&M general education areas (MATH, NQR, CSI, ALV, ARTS).

2) File the Letter of Intent (LOI)

  • Eligible after you complete ≥ 15 post-high-school VCCS credits with overall GPA ≥ 3.4.
  • Deadlines: Feb 1 (for fall entry) • Sept 1 (for spring entry).
  • Submit the LOI here: W&M Letter of Intent form.

3) Stay GAA-eligible while finishing your degree

  • Maintain GPA ≥ 3.4 through application and degree conferral.
  • Continuous enrollment (excluding summers) until you enter W&M.
  • Earn at least 45 credits at a VCCS college after high school that apply to the degree.
  • Grades:
    • B or better in a transferable 200-level ENG (composition/writing).
    • B or better in courses you are using to satisfy W&M COLL proficiencies/domains (MATH, ALV, CSI, NQR, ARTS).
    • Only courses with C or better transfer for credit.

4) Apply to W&M

  • Submit the Common App for Transfer by Mar 1 (fall) or Oct 1 (spring).
  • Send official transcripts from all colleges (and high school if you have < 24 post-HS credits).
  • Provide course syllabi on request for transfer credit evaluation.

5) After you’re admitted (what remains at W&M)

  • You’ll enter with junior standing (credit caps/degree rules apply).
  • Minimum 60 credits in residence at W&M are required to graduate.
  • Typical COLL items still expected:
    • Foreign Language proficiency through the 202 level (if not met before matriculation).
    • ARTS proficiency (2 credits) if not met before matriculation.
    • COLL 150 (taken at W&M) and 3 credits of COLL 200 in residence (9 credits waived).
    • COLL 300 (may be waived depending on pre-matriculation credit; check catalog) and COLL 400 (must be completed at W&M).
  • Declare your major on the W&M timeline (usually by end of first semester at W&M).

FAQ (quick hits)

Does the GAA cover every major? It guarantees university admission if you meet the agreement; some programs (e.g., Business/Education) have separate admissions or prerequisites.

Do AP/IB or dual-enrollment credits count toward the “15 credits” for the LOI? No — the LOI threshold is based on credits earned after high school at a VCCS college.

What if I miss the LOI deadline? You can still apply as a regular transfer, but the GAA guarantee won’t apply.

Tip: keep a running list of your completed VCCS courses, grades, and the W&M areas they satisfy. Bring syllabi for any course whose equivalency isn’t obvious.
